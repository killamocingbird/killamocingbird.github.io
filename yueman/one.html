Long distance is, I believe, one of the hardest things to do. I've given it lots of thought and I realized that you, probably like me, easily forget both past people and our disposition towards them. For me, I've realized quite early that I tend to forget things that seemingly "matter" quite often. No, not things like my phone. I mean things that matter to life -- connections, people, memories. I can pretty much remember history and math lessons taught to me back in elementary school, but I forget the people I meet almost instantaneously. Perhaps this is a defense mechanism? Who knows? All I do know is that this will be very hard for me. But I've committed to trying -- really trying with all my might. Even though I held you in my arms just 24 hours ago, I have a hard time imagining that I can survive all the way to 2020 without you. Perhaps this "gift" I am giving you is equally as important to me as I deemed it is to you. I want this to be different. I don't want to forget you. I won't forget you.

I can't shake the feeling that we're being foolish. When did you turn into more of a romantic than me? Sometimes I wonder how you can be so certain. I think a big reason I didn't want to do long distance was because I'd have my faith in myself shaken. I'd miss you so much that my brain would start to consider things more skeptically.

My sister asked me the other night, 《你真的了解她吗？》 I thought on this for a long time. I realized that while I am a rather very open individual, many times it takes me a long time to truly understand someone. To piece together what made them who they are, how they view the world, and my own perceptions of how they present themselves. But, 悦蔓, why is it that after spending this little time with you, I feel like I've known you for years? It is this feeling, this feeling of familiarity, that made my heart tremble that night at the bar. It is this feeling that makes me doubt not us, but the sanity of those who say it is impossible. It is this feeling that keeps me up at night remembering your smile, your laugh, and dumb habits. You tell me you still don't know what it means to love someone. To be honest I don't even know anymore either. What I feel for you is miles apart what I've felt for anyone else. In Beijing I thought that this must have been hormones in the moment type thing. But thinking back on you, even thousands of miles away, makes my heart flutter. That's so special to me. So, even if we don't make it. Even if you find another guy who is a better and closer version of me. You'll always have a place close in my heart, following me everywhere I go.
