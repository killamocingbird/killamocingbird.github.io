I've always wanted to see the fourth dimension visualized using color. However, I started to think about it and how to understand, or rather, "undo" an orthogonal projection into color (of the fourth dimension). I played around with this idea in MATLAB for visualization, and wanted to share the results with you! First, let's begin with an example of our ability to "undo" the projection of 3D onto 2D.


In this way, it is not hard to animate the frames in which the image on the left transitions to the image on the right. We have a mental understanding of how to "undo" the projection. Earlier I said to you how I would love to visualize the 4th dimension using such a projection, however, it wouldn't be beautiful at all. It would just look like a colored 3D curve, because the true beauty comes from our understanding of how to "undo" the dimension, which as 3D creatures, limits us from understanding 3D projections of the 4th dimension. See the example below.


The left and right show the same graph but with higher resolution on the right. These two graphs can be viewed as if it were the previous left graph. In other words, this 3D graph is a projection of a 4D graph. Unfortunately, because we are 3D creatures, I can't visualize the 4 dimensions in MATLAB, and even if I could, it would not be understandable. To better see what's going on, it may be helpful to imagine that the colors represent an orthogonal dimension which when isolated to from the original 3D shape looks like below.


It is important to note that color in the context of the first and third set of images correspond to the height or 3rd dimension (that way when it is collapsed into a 2D projection, we can imagine it in our heads as 3D). However, in the second set of graphs, color is used to show the 4th orthogonal spatial dimension, which is isolated in the last image. Even though it may be beautiful to imagine the 4th dimension, or to model it using color on a 3D projection, I found that we are bound by our limited brains. We cannot and will never truly be able to fathom, visually, the beauty of higher dimensions. Any thoughts on the subject must happen with cognitive dissonance or suspension of disbelief.

The closest thing I found to seeing the beauty of the 4th dimension can be found here. To think that scientists like Einstein were able to come up with a theory grounded in a 4th dimension is amazing. The link above is a short but fascinating read.
